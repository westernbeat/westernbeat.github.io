A little about myself

My name is James. Although, I prefer to be addressed as Jim. I was born in Melbourne, Australia in 1983 and raised in Sydney until the age of 15. My only spoken language is English however, I can communicate with people from the deaf community using some basic AUSLAN (Australian sign language). This is due to the kindness of Latrobe university who funded a private tutor to come to my home weekly for 20 weeks to say thanks for allowing my profoundly deaf daughter to participate in a study into the development of children with Cochlear Implants.

In 1999 I left school mid-way through year 10 to begin an apprenticeship. I am now a qualified plasterer after completing my Certificate III General Construction (Wall and Ceiling lining) in 2003. I attended Northern Melbourne Institute of TAFE(NMIT) Now known as Melbourne Polytechnic.

Immediately after completing my apprenticeship, I joined the Australian Army as a Gun Number (Gunner). A Gunner is responsible for the maintenance and operation of the Artillery Gun. In case you’re wondering, the primary objective of the Artillery is to support the infantry with indirect firepower. A Gunner is also referred to as a ‘nine-mile sniper’ can hit a 40-gallon drum from up to 15km away from the target.

When did my interest in IT start

My interest in IT began as a young child with my commodore 64. I have never been afraid to try things and always felt comfortable interacting with technology. I have broken many computers in the process and always investigated and hopefully repaired the problem.

What is my interest in IT?

Programming is my passion. In 2018 and with no programming experience, I began Investigating the possibility that I could develop an application myself because I could never seem to find one that met my needs. Although there are so many great applications on the market, I found myself using many of which only met a specific purpose.

I spent the first 3 months completely frustrated that I couldn’t seem to understand programming. There were times I began to believe I wasn’t intelligent enough and I almost quit. Rather than toss the idea into the too hard basket, I continued to persist and eventually things began to make sense.

What sparked my interest

In September 2018 I fractured my spine and had to rethink my career prospects. I was completely immobile. I became depressed, anxious and overwhelmed by my situation. My amazing ex-wife assisted me to explore options to keep my mind occupied and suggested I focus on programming while in recovery. Ultimately, I fell in love with programming which triggered an intense desire pursue a career in software development.

IT Experience

I have never been employed in an IT field. The extent of my experience stems no further than as an end user. I have experience using Invoicing applications, Microsoft office products. I have spent countless hours learning not only how to Program using different languages, but also the fundamentals of programming in general. I have completed SIT102 Introduction to Programming with Deakin College, and finished with a HD 95%. At the time I completed this unit the final result was determined by a learning portfolio only.

Why I chose to study at RMIT

Firstly, Due to my early life choices such as pursuing a trade qualification rather than graduating Secondary College. Secondly, moving 4 hours away from Melbourne, it became evident that my best option would be to withdraw from Deakin College and enroll with Open Universities. There are many Universities offering courses in IT. My decision to study with RMIT was based on the structure and the course content, offer by RMIT and whether I would connect with it. Furthermore, my goal is to earn a Bachelor of Computer Science. With limited pathways options and seemingly no online options, completing a Bachelor of Information Technology could create further development opportunities in the future.

I am enrolled in this course to further develop my knowledge and understanding of Information Technology. I understand that Information Technology is not just about programming and although essential, it is only one element of the field. While studying IT I expect to gain a solid understanding of the following topics.

  •   - What is Information Technology.
  •   - Why do we use Information Technology.
  •   - Where do we use Information Technology.
  •   - How we apply Information Technology.

By gaining a solid understanding of these ideas I have listed above, I expect to develop a solid understanding of the design, development and application of software and software systems.
